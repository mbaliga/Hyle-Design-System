# Hyle — Ethos & Lineage
### The philosophical README for the Hyle design system

## TL;DR
- **Hyle is internally coherent because every lineage it draws on converges on one move: meaning is carried by *matter behaving*, not by *language describing*.** Aristotle's hylomorphism (form emerging from matter), Weiser's calm technology (the periphery informs without speaking), Gibson's affordances (a surface specifies its own use), and the "truth to materials" tradition (Bauhaus, Rams, brutalism) all say the same thing in different vocabularies — which is exactly Hyle's core law: *state is shown by material behavior, never said by language.*
- **The hardest tensions are real and must be designed against, not waved away:** (a) calm vs. spectacle — a ferrofluid that erupts into spikes is attention-grabbing, the opposite of "disappearing"; (b) honesty vs. simulation — a *rendered* ferrofluid is by definition not "true to its material," so Hyle's honesty must be relocated from substance to *lawfulness of behavior*; (c) colour-coded provenance (radium-green vs. alien-cyan) collides with WCAG's rule that colour must never be the sole carrier of meaning.
- **The resolution is the load-bearing idea of the whole system:** Hyle is not honest about being glass or iron; it is honest about being *computation*. Light is only spent where thinking happens; the surface stays fixed while depth changes; motion occurs only when there is real state. Honesty-of-behavior is the through-line that lets the philosophy, the perception science, and the aesthetics cohere.

## Key Findings
1. **The name is a thesis.** *Hyle* (ὕλη) is Aristotle's word for matter — pure potentiality awaiting form (*morphē*). Naming an AI design system "matter" and then insisting form *emerges from material behavior* is a literal restatement of hylomorphism: the substrate (silica/silicon) is potential; the computation is the form that actualizes it.
2. **Calm technology is the spine, and the primary sources support "show, don't say" almost verbatim.** Amber Case's Principle #5 is literally "Technology can communicate but doesn't need to speak" — Hyle's "no status words, no spinners" is a direct application.
3. **Affordance theory tells Hyle which intellectual ancestor it actually uses.** Hyle leans Gibsonian (direct perception of a surface's possibilities) in ambition but is necessarily Normanian (designed *signifiers*) in practice — and being honest about that distinction is part of pressure-testing it.
4. **"Truth to materials" is the oldest root and the biggest trap.** The Arts-and-Crafts/Bauhaus/Rams lineage gives Hyle its moral vocabulary, but it also exposes Hyle's deepest contradiction: these are simulated materials.
5. **The aesthetic movements are not decoration; they are arguments.** Brutalism (honest mass), SEM imagery (the sublime of the very small made monumental), cymatics (frequency makes the pattern), and ferrofluid physics (form only under a field) each independently encode "behavior reveals state."
6. **The accessibility tension is the most concrete risk.** Provenance-by-colour and a near-black inky field are on a collision course with WCAG AA; the fix is redundant non-colour encoding.

## Details

### 1. Philosophical / Material Lineage

#### 1.1 Aristotle's *hyle* and hylomorphism
Aristotle (384–322 BCE) analyzed every concrete substance as a compound of **matter (*hylē*, ὕλη — literally "wood" or "timber") and form (*morphē* / *eidos*)**. Matter is the principle of *potentiality* — "that out of which" a thing is made; form is the principle of *actuality* that organizes matter into a determinate kind of thing. [Dalvoy](https://www.dalvoy.com/en/upsc/mains/previous-years/2025/philosophy-paper-i/aristotle-matter-form-empirical-world) He developed this in the *Physics* and *Metaphysics*, primarily to explain *change*: when bronze becomes a statue, the matter persists while the form changes (Physics I.7–9). **Prime matter** is the limiting concept — pure potentiality, a wholly indeterminate substrate with no actuality of its own.

The doctrine was extended by Thomas Aquinas (the soul as the substantial form of the body, in *De Ente et Essentia*) and survives today as **contemporary/neo-Aristotelian hylomorphism**, which analyzes objects as "structured composites whose identity and powers depend on both their material components and their organizing forms." There is a genuine philosophical *tension* worth importing as intellectual honesty: critics argue prime matter is "an empty concept, explaining nothing" — if the substratum persists through change it's merely accidental change; if it doesn't, it's creation from nothing. [Academia.edu](https://www.academia.edu/1611012/Hylomorphism_in_Aristotles_Physics) Aristotle's defenders (e.g. in *Physics* I.9) answer with "pure potentiality-for-substance." [Academia.edu](https://www.academia.edu/1611012/Hylomorphism_in_Aristotles_Physics)

**Why it matters for Hyle:** The system's "one substance, multiple grades" (sand → glass → silicon) is hylomorphism made literal. Silica is the *hyle* (potential); refinement is the imposition of *form*; the grade "where it computes" (crystalline silicon) is the *actualization*. The substance-story is also chemically real: quartz sand (SiO₂) is reduced with carbon at ~2000 °C to metallurgical silicon (~98–99% pure), then refined to hyperpure silicon for wafers; fused silica/quartz glass is >99.995% SiO₂. Sand, glass, and the silicon that computes are genuinely *the same element family at different grades of refinement* — so "glass = refined silica" is not a poetic conceit but a material fact. Hyle's core law — form/state emerges from material behavior — is the design-language translation of "form actualizes the potential latent in matter."

#### 1.2 Calm technology and ubiquitous computing
Mark Weiser, Chief Technologist at Xerox PARC, coined "ubiquitous computing" (~1988) and, with John Seely Brown, "calm technology." His 1991 *Scientific American* essay "The Computer for the 21st Century" opens: **"The most profound technologies are those that disappear. They weave themselves into the fabric of everyday life until they are indistinguishable from it."** [calmtech](https://calmtech.com/papers/computer-for-the-21st-century) Weiser uses writing/literacy as the model: "The constant background presence of these products of 'literacy technology' does not require active attention, but the information to be conveyed is ready for use at a glance." [calmtech](https://calmtech.com/papers/computer-for-the-21st-century)

In "The Coming Age of Calm Technology" (Weiser & Brown, Xerox PARC, October 5, 1996), the concept is defined by its relationship to attention: **"Calm technology engages both the center and the periphery of our attention, and in fact moves back and forth between the two."** [calmtech](https://calmtech.com/papers/coming-age-calm-technology) They define periphery as "what we are attuned to without attending to explicitly" [calmtech](https://calmtech.com/papers/coming-age-calm-technology) (the engine-noise-while-driving example), and argue "the periphery is informing without overburdening." [calmtech](https://calmtech.com/papers/coming-age-calm-technology) Their stated goal — "The result of calm technology is to put us at home, in a familiar place" [calmtech](https://calmtech.com/papers/coming-age-calm-technology) — maps directly onto Hyle's sovereignty thesis (on-device is "home").

**Amber Case** revived and codified this in *Calm Technology: Principles and Patterns for Non-Intrusive Design* (O'Reilly, 2015), expanding the PARC ideas into eight principles. The most load-bearing for Hyle: **#5, "Technology can communicate but doesn't need to speak"** (canonical text has no comma after "communicate"); #3, "Technology should make use of the periphery"; [calmtech](https://calmtech.com/) #7, "The right amount of technology is the minimum needed to solve the problem." [calmtech](https://calmtech.com/) Case's Principle #1 sub-point — "Consider how your technology communicates status" [calmtech](https://calmtech.com/) — is precisely Hyle's design problem. (Note: the widely circulated phrase "that which informs but doesn't demand our focus or attention" is a later paraphrase, not verbatim Weiser & Brown 1996; their actual phrasing is "will offer, but not demand.") [calmtech](https://calmtech.com/papers/coming-age-calm-technology)

**Critique to keep honest:** Yvonne Rogers, in "Moving on from Weiser's Vision of Calm Computing: Engaging UbiComp Experiences" (UbiComp 2006, LNCS 4206, pp. 404–421), argued that "progress in UbiComp research has been hampered by intractable computational and ethical problems… we need to rethink the value and role of calm and proactive computing as main driving forces" (p. 406), calling instead for a shift "from proactive computing to proactive people." The lesson for Hyle: calm is hard to actually deliver, and a system that *automates* calmness without keeping the user in control tends to fail; Hyle keeps the human as the prime mover.

#### 1.3 Skeuomorphism and its critique
A **skeuomorph** is "a derivative object that retains ornamental design cues from structures that were necessary in the original" [Medium](https://medium.com/@raveeshbhalla/understanding-material-design-7991b1469a63) — the term predates computing (archaeology used it for clay vessels imitating metal forms). Apple's early iOS (through iOS 6) used rich skeuomorphism (leather, linen, felt, glossy buttons); Microsoft's Metro (Windows 8, ~2012) and Apple's iOS 7 (2013) drove the flat-design backlash, as did Google's Material Design (2014). The honest reason flat won (per NN/g analysis) was not fashion: "textures cost screen space and rendering budget, dated quickly, and stopped being necessary once users no longer needed physical metaphors to understand touchscreens." [Superdesign](https://www.superdesign.dev/styles/skeuomorphism)

The crucial insight for Hyle: the strongest critics argue **flat design is not the opposite of skeuomorphism** — iOS 7+ retained shadows, parallax, blur, and physics-based motion. The real evolution is **from imitating an object's *look* to honoring a material's *behavior*.** Apple's "Liquid Glass" (announced WWDC 2025, shipped in iOS 26/macOS Tahoe) — "refraction, specular highlights, materials that run 'ultraclear to fully tinted'" [Superdesign](https://www.superdesign.dev/styles/skeuomorphism) — is the current expression of the same instinct in a new material: "Skeuomorphism made pixels behave like leather and brushed steel; glassmorphism and Liquid Glass make them behave like glass." [Superdesign](https://www.superdesign.dev/styles/skeuomorphism) Hyle inherits this directly but adds a discipline Liquid Glass lacks: behavior must encode *state*, not just look pretty.

**How Hyle avoids skeuomorphism's failures:** classic skeuomorphism lied — it imitated leather for decoration. Hyle's rule that "materials are assigned by meaning, not decoration" is the anti-skeuomorphic safeguard: glass means legibility, ferrofluid means live control, and nothing is textured for nostalgia.

#### 1.4 "Truth to materials" / material honesty
The principle runs from John Ruskin and the **Arts and Crafts** movement through Frank Lloyd Wright, the **Bauhaus** ("truth to materials" as a central teaching — supportive materials like steel should be exposed, not hidden), [Daevas Inc.](https://daevasdesign.com/bauhaus-truth-materials-central-principle/) to **Dieter Rams' sixth principle: "Good design is honest" (Gutes Design ist ehrlich)** — "It does not make a product appear more innovative, powerful or valuable than it really is." [Zack](https://zack.haus/en/blogs/germandesign/rams-principle-06-honest) The German concept **Materialgerechtigkeit** (material appropriateness) is the ethic of "treating materials as they are, without making them pretend to be something else" [Zack](https://zack.haus/en/blogs/germandesign/rams-principle-06-honest) — Rams explicitly rejected applying metallic paint to plastic or woodgrain prints to fiberboard. [Zack](https://zack.haus/en/blogs/germandesign/rams-principle-06-honest)

**Google Material Design (2014, Matías Duarte)** is the most direct precedent and foil. Its first principle, **"material is the metaphor,"** states: "The material is grounded in tactile reality, inspired by the study of paper and ink, yet technologically advanced and open to imagination and magic… the flexibility of the material creates new affordances that supercede those in the physical world, without breaking the rules of physics." [SlideShare](https://www.slideshare.net/limaniBhavik/google-material-design) Material Design's "ink cannot leave the paper," [Team Treehouse](https://teamtreehouse.com/library/getting-started-with-material-design/material-is-the-metaphor) elevation measured in dp, and "motion provides meaning" are all behavior-encodes-state moves. **Critique:** some argue Material Design is "inherently skeuomorphic" [Medium](https://medium.com/designed-thought/googles-material-design-24af1f50a816) (paper/ink is still a real-world metaphor); others note it looks to nature for meaning rather than man-made relics. [Medium](https://medium.com/designed-thought/googles-material-design-24af1f50a816) Hyle goes further than Material: where Material's metaphor is paper (passive, inert), Hyle's metaphor is silica-becoming-silicon (active, computational) — the material itself *is* the thinking.

**The unavoidable tension:** "truth to materials" demands real materials. Hyle's materials are rendered. A rendered ferrofluid is, strictly, a *lie about iron*. This is addressed in the Synthesis.

### 2. Perceptual / Design-Science Lineage

#### 2.1 Gibson's affordances vs. Norman's signifiers
**James J. Gibson** (*The Senses Considered as Perceptual Systems*, 1966; *The Ecological Approach to Visual Perception*, 1979) introduced **affordances**: action possibilities the environment offers an animal, perceived *directly* — without cognitive mediation or internal representation [ResearchGate](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/334715286_A_Review_of_Affordances_and_Affordance-Based_Design_to_Address_Usability) — via information in the **ambient optic array** and its **invariants**. Affordances are relational (depend on the animal's body and capabilities) but exist whether or not they are noticed: "We do not create affordances when we perceive them; they already exist in the system as constant relations between organism and environment." [Frontiers](https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/psychology/articles/10.3389/fpsyg.2018.02228/full)

**Don Norman** imported the term to design in *The Psychology of Everyday Things* (1988, later *The Design of Everyday Things*). Norman later regretted the looseness and split the concept: in "Signifiers, not affordances" (*Interactions*, 2008) and the 2013 revision he wrote: **"Affordances determine what actions are possible. Signifiers communicate where the action should take place. We need both."** [Sword STEM](https://swordstem.com/2023/08/02/part-3-affordances-introduction-to-ecological-approaches-for-coaching/) Ecological psychologists object that Norman's "perceived affordances" muddles Gibson's precise claim that perception is direct (rendering "perceived affordances" redundant — "perceived perceptions"). [arxiv](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2003.02307) William Gaver (1991) extended Gibson into HCI [TheoryHub](https://open.ncl.ac.uk/theories/22/affordances-theory/) with **perceptible, hidden, and false affordances**.

**Which does Hyle use?** Honestly, both — and naming the split is the pressure-test. Hyle *aspires* to Gibsonian directness: a still black ferrofluid mirror *is* "at rest"; spikes *are* "active" — perceived directly, no label. But because these are designed, on-screen, learned conventions, Hyle is in practice deploying **Norman's signifiers** (and Gaver's *perceptible affordances*). The intellectual integrity move is to admit Hyle manufactures signifiers engineered to *feel* like direct affordances.

#### 2.2 Material perception and why physical light reads as "real"
**Roland Fleming** (Giessen) and colleagues study how the visual system infers material properties. Key findings Hyle relies on: **specular highlights** are the single most important image cue for gloss (Marlow, Kim & Anderson 2012; Beck & Prazdny 1981); specular reflections behave *unlike* surface markings — they slide and distort as the object/viewer/light moves, and the visual system exploits this instability to read gloss and 3D shape (Fleming, Dror & Adelson). For transparent solids, the **distortion field** (refraction warping the background) plus Fresnel-driven specular reflection let the brain estimate refractive index. Fleming & Storrs (2019) showed an unsupervised neural network trained only to compress images of glossy surfaces spontaneously learned to "disentangle" lighting from reflectance and predicted human gloss *illusions* — evidence that material perception is statistical inference from image structure.

**Why it matters:** Hyle's insistence on "refraction over transparency" and "physically-accurate light" is perceptually well-founded. The eye reads correct specular + refractive behavior as *real material*, even on a flat display, because those are exactly the invariants Fleming's work identifies. This is the perceptual engine that makes "show, don't say" legible at all.

#### 2.3 Haptics, pseudo-haptics, and "wanting to touch"
**Hiroshi Ishii** (MIT Media Lab, Tangible Media Group) presented **"Tangible Bits"** with Brygg Ullmer at CHI '97 (DOI 10.1145/258549.258715) — giving "physical form to digital information," to transcend "Painted Bits" (GUIs). The vision: make bits "directly manipulable with our hands, and perceptible at the periphery of human awareness" (note the explicit calm-technology link to periphery), later extended to **Radical Atoms** (2012). Ullmer & Ishii's framework: physical representations are computationally coupled to digital information and embody mechanisms for interactive control.

**Pseudo-haptics** (Lécuyer et al., 2000; "Feeling bumps and holes without a haptic interface," CHI 2004) show that *vision alone* can induce haptic sensation — adjusting cursor speed over a texture makes users feel relief that isn't there. Lederman & Abbott (1981) established visual–tactile interplay in texture perception. **Why it matters for Hyle:** the "want to touch" quality of frosted glass and ferrofluid is a real, exploitable perceptual phenomenon — visual texture invites touch, and pseudo-haptic cues (subtle deformation on press, frost clearing at the contact patch) can make a flat AMOLED surface feel materially present without any haptic hardware.

### 3. Adjacent Aesthetic Movements (why they cohere)

#### 3.1 Brutalism
From Le Corbusier's **béton brut** ("raw concrete," e.g. Unité d'Habitation, Marseille, 1947–52). The term "New Brutalism" was coined by **Alison and Peter Smithson** (1953, for the unbuilt Soho house; first built example Hunstanton School, 1954) and theorized by critic **Reyner Banham** ("The New Brutalism," 1955; *The New Brutalism: Ethic or Aesthetic?*, 1966). Peter Smithson defined its core as "a reverence for materials… the seeing of materials for what they were: the woodness of the wood; the sandiness of sand." [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brutalist_architecture) It was, per the Smithsons, "an ethic, not an aesthetic." [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brutalist_architecture) Hallmarks: monumental mass, exposed structure, "as found" materials, honest display of services.

**Digital revival:** "Brutalist Web Design" (the manifesto site brutalist-web.design) and the broader trend explicitly invoke "truth to materials" and "honesty of materials — show the web for what it truly is." Its principles: function over form, content-first, raw structure exposed.

**Critique (essential to import):** brutalism is widely read as "cold," "inhuman," "oppressive"; many estates (e.g. Robin Hood Gardens) were demolished. **The Hyle tension:** brutalism's coldness directly contradicts Hyle's desire for controls you "want to touch." Hyle's answer is "soft brutalism" — monumental honesty of mass and material, warmed by tactility (the kiss of coloured light, the invitation of frosted glass).

#### 3.2 SEM aesthetics and the sublime of the small
Scanning electron microscope (SEM) images are **inherently greyscale** — "color is a property of light (photons), and since electron microscopes use an electron beam… there's no color information recorded." Any colour is **false colour** added afterward ("a product of human interpretation"). SEM images of microstructures, insects, and silicon dies famously read as "truly alien" monumental landscapes — vast civilizations within a tiny space. **Why it coheres:** this is the *mathematical sublime* (below) made microscopic, and it justifies Hyle's matte/greyscale-by-default, light-is-scarce field. The honesty point is sharp: real SEM is colorless and matte; Hyle's near-black, light-only-where-thinking-happens field is *truer to the actual physics of electron imaging* than false-colored science illustrations are.

#### 3.3 Cymatics
**Ernst Chladni** (1756–1827), "father of acoustics," published *Entdeckungen über die Theorie des Klanges* (1787), using a violin bow on sand-covered metal plates to reveal **Chladni figures** — sand collecting along nodal lines of standing waves. **Hans Jenny** (1904–1972), Swiss physician, coined **"cymatics"** (*Kymatik*) and published *Cymatics: The Study of Wave Phenomena* (vol. 1, 1967; vol. 2, 1972), using a tonoscope and crystal oscillators: low tones → simple patterns, higher tones → more complex structures. The physics is classical: standing waves, modes, antinode-to-node particle transport (related: Faraday waves). **Pseudoscience caution:** Jenny was a follower of Rudolf Steiner's anthroposophy, [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hans_Jenny_(cymatics)) and cymatics is sometimes co-opted for unfounded "sound healing" / "water memory" claims — Hyle should cite the *physics* (frequency → nodal pattern), not the mysticism.

**Why "frequency makes the pattern in the sand" is the right metaphor for cognition:** in Hyle, processing intensity (the "frequency" of computation) literally organizes the cymatic sand pattern behind the glass. Higher intensity → more complex pattern. It is a physically grounded, non-verbal display of thinking — pure "show, don't say."

#### 3.4 Ferrofluid
Invented 1963 by **NASA's Steve Papell** (patent 1965, US3215572A) as a way to pump rocket fuel in zero-g via magnetic field; the physics was elaborated by **Ronald E. Rosensweig**, who founded **ferrohydrodynamics** (*Ferrohydrodynamics*, 1985) and Ferrofluidics Corp (1968). A ferrofluid is nanoscale magnetite particles (~10 nm) in a carrier liquid with surfactant. At rest it is a flat black mirror; under a vertical magnetic field exceeding a critical value its surface spontaneously forms a hexagonal array of spikes — the **Rosensweig / normal-field instability** (Cowley & Rosensweig 1967), arising from the competition between magnetic force (destabilizing) and gravity + surface tension (stabilizing).

**Why it is the perfect "live control" material:** ferrofluid is iron — of-the-earth, elemental — and it is *physically incapable of forming spikes without an applied field.* It only deforms when a field (computation) is present. A still black mirror = idle; spikes = active. This is "show, don't say" enforced by physics itself: the material cannot lie about whether a field is present. Touching it = interrupting the field.

#### 3.5 Grain, the sublime, and monumentality
**Edmund Burke** (*A Philosophical Enquiry into the Origin of Our Ideas of the Sublime and Beautiful*, 1757) located the sublime in vastness, darkness, obscurity, and power — "delightful terror." **Immanuel Kant** (*Critique of the Power of Judgment*, 1790, §§25–28) distinguished the **mathematical sublime** (sheer magnitude that "defies measurement or complete understanding," exceeding our sensory grasp) from the **dynamical sublime** (overwhelming power). Crucially for Hyle, Kant locates the sublime *in the mind, not the object*: confronting vastness, reason discovers its own supersensible capacity — so the experience is, on balance, *empowering and calming*, not merely terrifying. Modern scholarship (Brady 2013) and psychology of *awe* (Keltner & Haidt's "vastness + need for accommodation") corroborate the link.

**Why grain + scale + one light = the Hyle mood:** heavy film/halftone grain reads as atmosphere and "realness"; a single key light from upper-left produces the "lone observer" mood; vast slow forms produce the mathematical sublime. Kant's insight — that vastness ultimately *calms* because the mind rises to meet it — is the philosophical justification for Hyle's signature claim: **"calm because massive."** The sovereignty signal (deep thinking = huge, slow, deliberate forms filling the frame) is the mathematical sublime deployed as a feeling of being safely at home inside one's own mind.

### 4. Synthesis — One Thesis

All eleven lineages converge on a single proposition: **a material's lawful behavior under forces is a more truthful, lower-attention, more directly-perceived carrier of state than any word.**

- **Matter (hylomorphism)** says form emerges from material actualizing its potential → state *is* the material taking form.
- **Calm technology** says the periphery should inform without speaking → no status words; "technology can communicate but doesn't need to speak."
- **Affordances (Gibson)** say a surface directly specifies its possibilities → ferrofluid-at-rest specifies "idle" without a label.
- **Truth to materials (Rams, Bauhaus, brutalism)** says don't make a thing pretend → assign materials by meaning, never decoration.
- **Material perception (Fleming)** says the eye reads correct light/refraction as *real* → physically-accurate rendering makes the behavior legible.
- **Cymatics & ferrofluid physics** say a field/frequency *makes* the pattern → computation literally sculpts the visible form.
- **The sublime (Kant) + SEM + brutalist mass** say vast, raw, monumental form calms rather than agitates → "calm because massive."

The unifying claim — Hyle's real intellectual contribution — is that **honesty is relocated from substance to behavior.** Hyle does not claim to be glass or iron. It claims to be *computation*, and it tells the truth about computation: light only where thinking happens; spikes only under a field; pattern only under frequency; mass proportional to depth of thought.

### 5. Pressure-Test (the tensions, engaged honestly)

- **(a) Calm vs. spectacle.** Weiser wants technology to *disappear*; ferrofluid spikes *demand* attention. Resolution: Hyle obeys Case's principle that calm technology "will move easily from the periphery of our attention, to the center, and back." Stillness is the default (matte black mirror); spectacle is *earned* and *brief*, occurring only when there is real state, then receding. If Hyle is spectacular at rest, it has failed.
- **(b) Honesty vs. simulation.** A rendered ferrofluid is not iron; "truth to materials" purists (Rams) would call it a lie. Resolution: Hyle is not honest about *being* the material; it is honest about the *laws* the material obeys. The ferrofluid must never spike without a "field" (real computation); the glass must refract correctly. The honesty is procedural, not substantial. This is the single most important idea to get right.
- **(c) Brutalist coldness vs. wanting to touch.** Resolution: import brutalism's *material honesty and mass*, not its *alienation*; warm it with tactility (pseudo-haptic press feedback, the "kiss" of light). "Soft brutalism."
- **(d) Repeating skeuomorphism's failure.** Skeuomorphism failed by decorating with dead metaphors. Hyle's safeguard: materials assigned strictly by meaning; no texture without a behavioral job.
- **(e) Accessibility vs. inky colour-coded aesthetic.** This is the most concrete risk. WCAG 1.4.1 (Use of Color) requires that colour is *never the only* means of conveying information — and congenital red–green colour blindness affects up to 1 in 12 males (8%) and 1 in 200 females (0.5%); per Birch (2012), "Worldwide prevalence of red-green color deficiency" (*J. Opt. Soc. Am. A*; PubMed 22472762), prevalence "in European Caucasians is about 8% in men and about 0.4% in women and between 4% and 6.5% in men of Chinese and Japanese ethnicity." Hyle's provenance system (radium-green = on-device vs. alien-cyan = cloud) violates this if colour alone carries it. Mandatory mitigations: pair hue with a *second non-colour channel* (form, motion signature, position, iconography, or texture), keep the near-black field at #121212-class rather than pure #000 to reduce halation, and verify ≥4.5:1 text / ≥3:1 non-text contrast per theme. The single violet accent (#8E7BFF) and WCAG AA "north star" must be enforced with real contrast testing, not visual judgment.
- **(f) Sublime awe vs. everyday usability.** A tool used hundreds of times a day cannot be overwhelming each time. Resolution: reserve the sublime (vast slow forms, zoom) strictly for genuine cognitive-depth changes; routine work stays small, fast, peripheral. Awe is rationed.
- **(g) Gibson vs. Norman.** Hyle aspires to Gibsonian direct perception but ships Normanian signifiers. Be honest: test whether users read states *without* training. Where they can't, the "physics" isn't yet doing its job and a clearer behavioral invariant — not a word — is the fix.

## Recommendations
1. **Adopt "honesty of behavior, not substance" as the constitutional clause** of the repo, directly under the core law. Every material must have a documented "law" (the force it responds to and the state that force represents). Stage gate: no material ships without its law written down.
2. **Make the accessibility mitigation non-optional and first-class.** Before any colour-coded provenance ships, implement a second non-colour channel for radium-green vs. alien-cyan, run automated contrast checks per theme (≥4.5:1 text, ≥3:1 UI/graphics), and simulate deuteranopia/protanopia. Threshold that changes the plan: if provenance fails a colour-blind simulation, the colour system is blocked until the redundant cue exists.
3. **Encode the calm/spectacle budget as a motion rule.** Default state must be matte and still; any "alive" carrier motion must be steady and peripheral (the BlackBerry-LED model); attention-grabbing deformation (spikes, colour-rise) is permitted only on real state change and must decay back to rest. Motion uses weight, not bounce — cubic-bezier(.4,0,.2,1), ~280–320 ms, no spring. Benchmark: if a reviewer can't tell idle from active *at a glance and in silence*, the behavior is wrong.
4. **Write the glossary (below) into the repo root** so contributors share exact terminology.
5. **Test Gibsonian directness empirically.** Run unlabeled comprehension tests: can a new user distinguish on-device vs. cloud, idle vs. thinking, routine vs. deep, *with no words on screen*? Track a "words required to explain a state" metric; target zero. Any state that needs a word is, by the core law, a design bug.
6. **Cite the physics, not the mysticism**, for cymatics and ferrofluid in all documentation, to keep the system intellectually defensible.

### Foundational Glossary
- **Hyle (ὕλη):** Aristotle's "matter," pure potentiality; the system's name and thesis — form/state emerges from material.
- **Hylomorphism:** matter (*hylē*) + form (*morphē*); the metaphysics behind "one substance, multiple grades."
- **Substance-story:** sand → fused/polished glass → crystalline silicon; one material (silica/SiO₂ → Si) at rising grades of refinement; refinement = the system working.
- **Calm technology (Weiser/Brown 1996; Case 2015):** tech that "engages both the center and the periphery… and moves back and forth"; "can communicate but doesn't need to speak."
- **Periphery / center:** Weiser's attention model; Hyle keeps state in the periphery until it earns the center.
- **Affordance (Gibson):** directly-perceived action possibility of a surface. **Signifier (Norman):** a designed cue indicating where action goes. Hyle uses signifiers engineered to feel like affordances.
- **Specular highlight / distortion field (Fleming):** the optical cues the eye uses to read gloss and refraction; the engine of "looks real."
- **Material honesty / Materialgerechtigkeit / "good design is honest" (Rams #6):** don't let a material pretend; in Hyle, relocated to honesty of *behavior*.
- **Béton brut / New Brutalism (Le Corbusier; Smithsons 1953; Banham 1955):** raw material + honest mass; "the sandiness of sand." Hyle = "soft brutalism."
- **Chladni figures / Cymatics (Chladni 1787; Jenny 1967):** frequency organizes particles into nodal patterns → cognition/processing display.
- **Rosensweig / normal-field instability (Cowley & Rosensweig 1967):** ferrofluid spikes only above a critical field → live control; idle = flat mirror.
- **Mathematical sublime (Kant 1790):** vastness that calms because the mind rises to meet it → "calm because massive."
- **False colour (SEM):** SEM is inherently greyscale; colour is added by hand. Justifies Hyle's matte, light-is-scarce field.
- **Provenance lights:** radium-like green = on-device/native ("home"); alien-cyan = cloud/foreign ("watched"). Light = computation; must be earned.
- **Core law:** *State is SHOWN by material behavior, never SAID by language.*

## Caveats
- **Primary-source precision:** the popular calm-tech line "informs but doesn't demand our focus or attention" is a paraphrase, not verbatim Weiser & Brown 1996; their exact phrasing is "will offer, but not demand." Case's Principle #5 is canonically "Technology can communicate but doesn't need to speak" (no comma after "communicate"). Use the verbatim forms.
- **Cymatics carries pseudoscience baggage** (Steiner anthroposophy, "sound healing," "water memory"). Only the classical-physics claims (standing waves, nodal patterns) are defensible.
- **Hylomorphism's prime matter is genuinely contested** in philosophy ("an empty concept"); Hyle borrows it as a productive metaphor, not a settled metaphysics.
- **The honesty-of-behavior resolution is a claim, not a proof.** A determined critic can still say all rendered materials are skeuomorphic illusions; Hyle's defense rests on procedural honesty (behavior always tied to real state), which must be enforced in code, or the critique lands.
- **Calm computing has a documented track record of over-promising** (Rogers 2006). Hyle mitigates this by keeping the human as prime mover rather than automating "calm" on the user's behalf.
- **Accessibility is the highest-probability real-world failure** and is treated as a hard gate above, not a nicety.
- Apple's "Liquid Glass" detail is recent (WWDC 2025) and its long-term reception is still unfolding; it is cited as a parallel instinct, not a validated success.
